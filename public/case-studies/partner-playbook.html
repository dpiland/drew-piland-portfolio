The Strategic Context

Why this playbook was built and what problem it solves
Internal Enablement
Background

A global enterprise technology company with a dominant hardware franchise made a strategic decision to grow recurring software and services revenue — bringing on 20+ vendor partnerships across security, productivity, unified endpoint management, education, and branded solutions in under two years.

The field sales organization had the customer relationships. What it lacked was a system: a clear prioritization of what to sell to whom, a common language for positioning, organized access to enablement resources, and a structured process for converting hardware conversations into software pipeline. This playbook was the answer.

The Problem
  • No unified framework for navigating 20+ offerings
  • No segment-based prioritization — everything felt like Tier 1
  • Sales enablement assets scattered across multiple systems
  • No repeatable process from lead identification to specialist handoff
  • Inconsistent positioning across partner and internal teams
The Solution
  • 5-pillar portfolio architecture with distinct value propositions
  • Priority tiering matrix by segment (T1/T2/T3 across 7 segments)
  • 6-stage sales support process with clear exit criteria
  • Organized resource snapshots by pillar and offering type
  • Conversation frameworks mapped to buyer pain → product solution
PMM role: Owned the GTM strategy, portfolio architecture, pillar value propositions, enablement infrastructure design, and launch of this playbook. Drove cross-functional alignment across 20+ vendor partner managers, sales leadership, and operations.

Exit Criteria 1 Qualifying conversation completed. Lead routed to software specialist using segment-based routing logic.
Exit Criteria 2 Software specialist joins call. Opportunity jointly scoped. Partner manager aligned on vendor solution.
Exit Criteria 3 Quote built through central system. Deal registration completed with vendor partner.
Exit Criteria 4 Opportunity closed-won. Onboarding call coordinated with vendor partner success team.

Exit Criteria 6 Vendor-specific support docs distributed. Account team maintains relationship context.
Routing Logic
Relationship-led accounts: Initial lead sent to account owner, who engages the software specialist overlay team.
Unassigned accounts: Lead routed to segment Services CAM, who engages the appropriate specialist resource.

Sales Enablement · Security Pillar

Conversation Starters: Security

Identify. Qualify. Schedule. — A guided discovery framework for field reps

Field reps don't need product expertise to open a software conversation. They need the right questions. This framework maps discovery questions to pain signals, and pain signals to product solutions — so every rep can qualify fast and hand off confidently.

Stage Questions to Ask Listen For…
Identify
  • What is your primary security goal for the next 12 months — and how would a breach impact that?
  • How have your security policies adapted to distributed and hybrid work arrangements?
  • How are you currently ensuring your endpoints are protected from anywhere?
  • Are you able to track, assess, and manage your full device fleet in real time?
  • Are users managing their own passwords? Do you have MFA deployed across the org?
 Lack of endpoint visibility / inability to remote wipe → Endpoint Persistence Platform No fleet-wide purchase or lifecycle data → Asset Inventory Manager Too much time patching drivers / 3rd-party apps → Automated Patch Management Exposure to ransomware / zero-day threats → Next-Gen EDR Password sprawl / high help-desk MFA costs → Passwordless MFA
Qualify
  • How are you managing firmware, driver, and third-party software updates today — and how long does it typically take?
  • Are there compliance frameworks you're working toward — NIST, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP?
  • If a ransomware event happened today, what's your rollback and recovery plan?
  • How many endpoints are you managing, and are they all on the same management stack?
 SCCM-managed environment → Integrated UEM / Automated Patch Management Regulatory compliance requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, FedRAMP) → Disk Encryption Platform No rollback capability → Next-Gen EDR (one-click recovery) Mixed or unmanaged device estate → Endpoint Persistence Platform
Schedule
  • "Our security portfolio is modular — we can design a solution specific to your exposure and compliance requirements."
  • "I'd like to bring in one of our security specialists to go deeper. When would work for you? Is there anyone else from your IT or security team we should include?"
 → Engage Security Solutions Specialist overlay team → Route through account owner for named accounts; through segment CAM for unassigned
Field reminder: Reps don't need to close software — they need to open the conversation and hand off cleanly. The goal of this framework is a qualified meeting booked with a specialist, not product expertise. Book the meeting.
